# Amazon SDE2 — Leadership Principles (Behavioral) Questions (Last 6 Months)

> Compiled from real interview experiences on **LeetCode Discuss**, **GeeksforGeeks**, **interviewexperiences.in**, **HelloInterview**, **Exponent**, **CrackingWalnuts**, **Medium**, and **LinkedIn** (~Dec 2025 – Jun 2026).

---

## Why LPs Matter So Much
- LP questions appear in **every single round** (typically **2 per round**, ~25 min each — sometimes **>50%** of a "technical" interview).
- Amazon weights behavioral signal **as heavily as** technical performance. A strong coder with weak LP signal gets **rejected**; a borderline coder with stellar LP stories can get hired.
- The **Bar Raiser** (cross-team, veto power) probes LPs deeply and checks **consistency** across rounds.
- Interviewers use an internal question bank (AIQB) and score answers in **STAR** structure.

---

## Reported LP Questions (grouped by principle)

### Dive Deep
- "Tell me about a time you had to investigate a technical issue deeply and identify the root cause."
- "What is your role when a job fails in production?"

### Bias for Action
- "Tell me about a time you had to make a decision quickly without complete information."

### Ownership
- "Tell me about a time you owned a module/project end-to-end, from conception to completion."
- "Tell me about a time you missed a tight deadline. How did you handle it?"

### Deliver Results
- "Describe a time you compromised on quality to meet a tight deadline and deliver quickly."

### Customer Obsession
- "Tell me about a time you went above and beyond for a customer."
- "Tell me about a time you simplified a complex process/feature to make it easier for a customer."

### Earn Trust
- "Describe a conflict or disagreement with your manager and how you resolved it."

### Invent and Simplify / Insist on the Highest Standards
- "Tell me about a time you identified an inefficiency and improved an existing process."

### Are Right, A Lot
- Follow-ups on calculated risk-taking and decisions defended with **data**.

### Other Reported Themes
- Delivering impact with **vague/ambiguous requirements**.
- Prioritizing **operational excellence vs. feature work**.
- Pushing back on **unrealistic timelines** (especially with the Hiring Manager).

## STAR Method & Tips
- **S**ituation → **T**ask → **A**ction → **R**esult. Interviewers are trained to score in this structure.
- Use **recent stories** (last **12–18 months**) — they score higher.
- Lead with **data/metrics** and **personal ownership** ("I", not "we").
- Each story should be tellable in **~90 seconds**, with room for follow-ups.
- Prepare stories that can **flex** across multiple principles.
- In technical rounds, **engage with LP questions in detail**, not in passing.
- **Communication & structured thinking** matter as much as the answer itself.
