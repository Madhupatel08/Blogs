# Salesforce SDE2 / MTS — Behavioral / Ohana Values Questions (Last 6 Months)

> Compiled from real interview experiences on **gitGood.dev**, **sirjohnnymai.com**, **Medium**, **LeetCode Discuss**, and **interviewexperiences.in** (~Dec 2025 – Jun 2026).

---

## Why Behavioral Matters at Salesforce
- Behavioral rounds are a **primary filter for cultural fit**, not a formality.
- Salesforce evaluates alignment with the **Ohana** philosophy (family/community) and **V2MOM** goal-setting framework.
- They look for **stewardship** (improving overall team health) over room-dominating "leadership."
- A **technically perfect but arrogant** candidate is rejected in debrief — the cost of a "toxic high-performer" is considered too high.
- You're judged on collaborating across **fragmented, cross-functional teams** with significant **legacy debt** — navigating bureaucracy with **empathy**, not frustration.

---

## Salesforce Core Values
1. **Trust** — security, reliability, transparency above all.
2. **Customer Success** — customer outcomes over individual heroics.
3. **Innovation** — continuous improvement.
4. **Equality** — inclusion and fairness.
5. **Sustainability** — long-term, responsible stewardship.

---

## Reported Behavioral Questions / Themes
- "**Why do you want to switch?**" / "**Why Salesforce?**"
- Project **deep-dive** — your **role and impact** (not just the team's).
- "Tell me about a time you made a **technical compromise**." (Map 3 past projects via STAR, highlighting trade-offs.)
- Navigating **cross-team / cross-functional** complexity and **legacy debt** with empathy.
- **Mentoring** others / improving overall **team health**.
- Handling **compliance / regulatory** constraints (SOC 2, HIPAA, FedRAMP) for some segments.
- "How do you use **AI** in your day-to-day work?"
- **Relocation** concerns / logistics.
- Long-running **customer relationships** and enterprise sensibilities.

---

## STAR Method & Tips
- Use **S**ituation → **T**ask → **A**ction → **R**esult.
- **Mindset shift:** move from "**I** built this" → "**We** enabled this." Emphasize collaboration and stewardship.
- Prepare **3 project stories** that each surface a **technical compromise / trade-off** and the reasoning behind it.
- Tie answers to **Ohana values** (Trust, Customer Success, Equality, Sustainability).
- Show **empathy** navigating bureaucracy and cross-team dependencies — frame frustration as constructive problem-solving.
- Demonstrate **customer-first** thinking and ownership of outcomes, not just code.
